About us

A Global Phenomenon Spanning Decades

Pokémon debuted in 1996 with the release of Pokémon Red and Green, quickly capturing the imaginations of fans worldwide. Starting as a video game series, it has since expanded into TV shows, movies, trading cards, and toys. Today, Pokémon is one of the most successful entertainment franchises, beloved by fans of all ages.

A Universe of Iconic Creatures

With over 1,000 different species of Pokémon, the franchise has created a vast world full of unique and lovable creatures. From Pikachu, the franchise's mascot, to legendary Pokémon like Mewtwo and Rayquaza, each one plays a role in the ongoing adventures of Pokémon trainers. Their stories and battles continue to captivate both casual fans and dedicated collectors.

A Cultural Touchstone Across Generations

Pokémon’s influence extends beyond games and shows—it has become a cultural icon. Its impact can be seen in fashion, mobile gaming (with Pokémon GO), and international events like the Pokémon World Championships. The franchise’s legacy spans multiple generations, with both longtime fans and new players continually discovering the joy of catching 'em all.
